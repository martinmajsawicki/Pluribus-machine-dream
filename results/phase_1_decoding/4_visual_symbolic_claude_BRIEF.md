## 5. THE TITLE: "PLURIBUS" AND "PLUR1BUS"

### The Inverted Motto

*E pluribus unum*: "Out of many, one"

**Original meaning:** Many states/peoples unite to form one nation. Diversity preserved within unity. The "one" is a *structure* containing the "many."

**Pluribus inversion:** Many consciousnesses dissolve into one mind. Diversity eliminated. The "one" *replaces* the "many."

**The missing "E" and "UNUM":** The show's title is just "PLURIBUS"â€”the many. But the many no longer exist. The title names what has been *lost*, not what remains. It's an elegy disguised as a name.

**American irony:** The U.S. motto on currency, on government buildings, represents the American ideal. Pluribus suggests that ideal's dark mirrorâ€”unity achieved through elimination of difference. The American dream of *chosen* unity becomes the nightmare of *imposed* unity.

### The "1" in "PLUR1BUS"

**Visual replacement:** The numeral "1" replaces the letter "I."

**Layers of meaning:**

1. **The singular consciousness:** One mind has replaced individual "I"s. The self is now a number.

2. **Dehumanization:** Letters are human language; numbers are data. The substitution visualizes humanity becoming *countable*, *quantifiable*, *processed*.

3. **The immune as "1"s:** Thirteen ones who haven't become part of the collective. They are the remaining "I"sâ€”or the remaining "1"s in a world of zeros.

4. **Binary suggestion:** 1 and 0. On and off. Joined and immune. The world reduced to binary states.

5. **The first/the singular:** "1" suggests primacy, origin. Is this the *first* Joining? Will there be others? Is Earth "Pluribus 1"?

6. **Hidden in plain sight:** The "1" draws the eye. It *announces* that something is wrong, replaced, artificial. The title itself is infected.

### Typography as Theme

The stylization "PLUR1BUS" performs the show's theme visually. Every time we see the title, we see:
